# The Output Is Not the Offering
## A word for the woman who has been mistaking her exhaustion for faithfulness

*Daily Devotion · Wednesday, May 27, 2026 · Profile rotation: The Striving Achiever*

---

You have been faithful. I want to say that first, before anything else.

You have woken early. You have stayed late. You have answered the emails and kept the calendar and held small fires from spreading. And somewhere along the way, the doing became the proof — the thing that told you you were still worth keeping.

> *"Come to me, all who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest."*
> — Matthew 11:28

He did not say earn rest. He said come.

Here is what I have been sitting with this morning. God has never been impressed by output. He has only ever been moved by nearness. The woman who collapses into Him empty-handed is closer to His heart than the woman who arrives with a finished list and an exhausted body.

You were not built to be a producer. You were built to be a daughter. The two are not the same thing.

Stillness is not the absence of obedience. It is the older form of it. Long before you were asked to do anything for Him, you were asked to be with Him. That is still the first thing. It is still the only thing.

Let today be the day you stop bringing Him what you have accomplished and start bringing Him what is actually here — your tired hands, your quiet body, the soul beneath the schedule. He has been waiting for that. Not for the version of you that has it together, but for the one who finally sets it down.
