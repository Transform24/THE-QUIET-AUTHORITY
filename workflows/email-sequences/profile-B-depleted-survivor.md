# Profile B — The Depleted Survivor
**Tagline:** You were meant to be the vessel, not the source.
**Scripture:** Psalm 23:2-3

---

## Email 1 — Send Immediately After Assessment
**Subject:** Your Silence Profile is here, [First Name]
**Preview text:** Empty isn't the end. It's the beginning.

---

[First Name],

You just completed The Quiet Authority assessment — and what it revealed about you is sacred.

You are **The Depleted Survivor.**

Here's what that means:

You're not just tired. You're empty. The kind of empty that sleep doesn't fix. The kind that wakes you at 3am with a heaviness you can't name. You've been giving from a well that ran dry a long time ago — and somehow you've kept going anyway.

That's not weakness. That's what happens when a woman carries what she was never meant to carry alone.

*"He leads me beside quiet waters. He restores my soul."*
— Psalm 23:2–3

He doesn't say you restore your own soul. He doesn't say try harder, push through, dig deeper. He says — *I will lead you. I will restore you.*

**Your first practice (start tonight):**
Set a timer for 5 minutes. Sit down. Don't try to fill the silence with prayer, with gratitude lists, with anything. Just breathe. Let the emptiness be the prayer. It already is.

You were meant to be the vessel, [First Name]. Not the source.

Let Him be the source tonight.

Walking with you,
**Grace**

## Email 2 — Send Day 3
**Subject:** What depletion is actually telling you
**Preview text:** Your emptiness is not a spiritual failure. It's an invitation.

---

[First Name],

I want to say something directly:

**Your depletion is not a spiritual failure.**

The enemy wants you to believe it is. He wants you to see your exhaustion as evidence that your faith isn't strong enough, your prayer life isn't deep enough, your trust isn't real enough.

That is a lie.

Your depletion is an invitation. It's the place where self-reliance finally collapses — and dependence on God becomes not a nice idea, but your only option. And that place? That is holy ground.

*"My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness."*
— 2 Corinthians 12:9

Perfect. In weakness. Not in spite of it. In it.

The women who know God most deeply are not the ones who had it all together. They are the ones who were brought to the end of themselves — and found Him there.

You are there, [First Name]. And He is there too.

**This week's practice:**
Each morning before you rise, place your hand on your chest and say out loud: *I don't have to generate this today. He is my source.* Then sit for 10 minutes. Just receive.

No agenda. No output. Just receive.

Still with you,
**Grace**

Wait.
