# Profile D — The Lost Wanderer
**Tagline:** Silence isn't where you lose yourself — it's where you remember.
**Scripture:** Psalm 46:10

---

## Email 1 — Send Immediately After Assessment
**Subject:** Your Silence Profile is here, [First Name]
**Preview text:** God knows your name. Even when you've forgotten it.

---

[First Name],

You just completed The Quiet Authority assessment — and what it revealed about you is more important than you may realize right now.

You are **The Lost Wanderer.**

Here's what that means:

You've been running — not from God, but from the noise. The roles. The expectations. The endless demands of a life that somehow became everyone else's and stopped feeling like yours. You wake up some mornings and wonder: *who am I when no one needs anything from me?*

And the silence? Silence feels scary because you're not sure who you'll find there.

I want to tell you something:

*"Before I formed you in the womb, I knew you."*
— Jeremiah 1:5

Before the roles. Before the responsibilities. Before the world told you who to be — He knew you. He named you. And that woman? She is still there. She has been waiting for you in the stillness.

**Your first practice (start tonight):**
Set a timer for 10 minutes. Sit somewhere quiet. Ask only this: *God, who did you create me to be before the world had a say?*

Don't force an answer. Just ask. Then listen. The silence is not empty — it is full of everything He's been trying to tell you.

Walking with you,
**Grace**

## Email 2 — Send Day 3
**Subject:** The woman beneath all the roles
**Preview text:** She's still there. She never left.

---

[First Name],

Three days ago you stepped into the silence and asked a question most women are afraid to ask:

*Who am I, really?*

I want to tell you what I know about women like you — women who have been carrying everyone else's version of them for so long they've lost the thread back to themselves:

**She's still there. The original you. She never left.**

She just got buried under the noise. Under the "mom." Under the "employee." Under the "reliable one" and the "strong one" and the "one who has it together." Under years of being needed before you were known.

*"I have called you by name; you are mine."*
— Isaiah 43:1

He didn't call you by your role. He called you by your name. *Your* name. The one He gave you before anyone else had a claim on your time, your energy, your identity.

**This week's practice:**
Find 12 minutes each morning this week. Sit in silence and ask: *God, what do you call me?* Not what others call you. Not what you call yourself on your worst days. What does He call you?

Write down whatever comes. Even if it feels small. Even if it feels like you're making it up. Write it down.

The woman you're looking for is not lost. She is being found.

Still with you,
**Grace**

Not dramatically. Not all at once. But in those quiet morning minutes, in the spaces between the roles and the demands — something in you has remembered. A flicker of the woman God created. A whisper of who you were before the world got loud.

*"Be still, and know that I am God."*
— Psalm 46:10

Be still. And *know.* The knowing comes in the stillness, [First Name]. Not in the striving. Not in the figuring out. In the still.

You are not who the noise says you are. You are not the sum of your roles. You are not defined by who needs you or what you produce or how long you can keep going.

You are a woman God knew before you were formed. And He is using this season — this disorientation, this wilderness — to lead you back to yourself.
