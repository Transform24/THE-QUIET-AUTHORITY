# Profile A — The Striving Achiever
**Tagline:** Your worth isn't in your output — it's in His love.
**Scripture:** Matthew 11:28

---

## Email 1 — Send Immediately After Assessment
**Subject:** Your Silence Profile is here, [First Name]
**Preview text:** You've been achieving. God's been waiting.

---

[First Name],

You just completed The Quiet Authority assessment — and what it revealed about you matters.

You are **The Striving Achiever.**

Here's what that means:

You've built something remarkable with your life. Your work ethic, your reliability, your ability to get things done — these are genuine gifts. But somewhere along the way, achieving became the price of admission for feeling worthy. And silence? Silence feels like a threat to everything you've constructed.

God sees it differently.

*"Come to me, all who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest."*
— Matthew 11:28

He's not asking you to perform. He's not waiting for you to earn your way into His presence. He is simply — and radically — inviting you to **stop.**

**Your first practice (start tonight):**
Set a timer for 5 minutes. Sit down. Do nothing. Accomplish nothing. When your mind races to the list of things undone, gently return to this one thought: *I am enough right now.*

Not because of what you did today. Because of whose you are.

That's where your breakthrough begins.

Walking with you,
**Grace**

## Email 2 — Send Day 3
**Subject:** The thing no one tells high achievers about rest
**Preview text:** Rest is not the opposite of faith. It's the practice of it.

---

[First Name],

Three days ago you discovered you're a Striving Achiever.

I want to tell you something that took me years to understand:

**Rest is not the absence of work. It's the practice of trust.**

When you stop — truly stop — you are making a declaration. You are saying: *God is working even when I am not.* You are saying: *His plans do not require my exhaustion to succeed.*

That is not laziness. That is faith in its most tangible form.

The enemy has lied to you. He's convinced you that your value lives in your output. That silence is irresponsible. That if you slow down, something essential will fall apart.

But Psalm 127:2 says: *"It is vain for you to rise up early, to sit up late, to eat the bread of sorrows — for He gives sleep to His beloved."*

**He gives it. As a gift. To His beloved.**

You are His beloved. Not His employee. Not His contractor. Not His top performer.

His beloved.

**This week's practice:**
Before you open your phone each morning, sit for 5 minutes. Ask only this: *Who am I before I produce anything today?*

Let the answer come slowly. It will.

Still walking with you,
**Grace**
