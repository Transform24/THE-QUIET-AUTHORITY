## Email 1 — Send Immediately
**Subject line:** Your Silence Profile is here, [First Name]
**Preview text:** The silence has been trying to tell you something.

---

[First Name],

You just did something most women won't do.

You stopped. You answered 8 honest questions. You let the silence have a moment.

That matters more than you know.

Whatever your Silence Profile revealed — whether you're striving, depleted, giving too much, or feeling lost — there is one truth underneath all of it:

**The silence you've been avoiding is the very place your breakthrough is waiting.**

*"Be still, and know that I am God."*
— Psalm 46:10

Not be still when things calm down. Not be still when the kids are grown or the schedule clears or life finally cooperates. Be still. Now. In the middle of all of it.

That's what The Quiet Authority is really about — not the absence of noise, but learning to find God in the middle of it.

**Your first practice (start tonight):**

Set a timer for 5 minutes. Sit somewhere quiet. Put your phone face down. Close your eyes and breathe slowly.

Don't try to pray perfectly. Don't try to feel anything specific. Just show up.

That's it. Five minutes. Just show up.

The woman who does this — who keeps showing up to the silence even when it feels awkward, even when her mind races, even when nothing dramatic happens — that woman gets transformed.

I'll be walking this with you.

With love,
**Grace**
*Sanctuary Grace Ministry*

P.S. Your full results are on your assessment — including your personalized 7-day silence practice. Return to it this week and begin.

## Email 2 — Send Day 3
**Subject line:** Three days in — what is the silence saying?
**Preview text:** It's already speaking. Here's how to hear it.

---

[First Name],

Three days ago you took the assessment. I want to check in.

Not because you need to report your progress. Not because there's a right answer. But because the silence you've been sitting in — even for just a few minutes — has been speaking.

And I don't want you to miss it.

Here's what I know about the women who take this assessment:

Most of them have been running on autopilot for years. Not bad years. Full years. Meaningful years. But years where the pace became the point, and somewhere in the rushing, the voice of God got quieter and quieter — not because He stopped speaking, but because the noise got louder.

The silence practice isn't about emptying your mind. It's about **making room for the one voice that matters most.**

*"My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me."*
— John 10:27

You were made to hear His voice, [First Name]. Not just read about it. Not just hear it in a sermon once a week. Hear it — personally, intimately, daily.

That's what 5 minutes of silence is actually training you to do.

**This week's practice:**
When you sit in silence, bring one question: *God, what do you want me to know today?*

Then wait. Breathe. Don't rush the answer. Sometimes it comes as a word. Sometimes a feeling. Sometimes a memory. Sometimes just a deep, settling peace.

All of it counts. All of it is Him.

Keep showing up.

**Grace**
