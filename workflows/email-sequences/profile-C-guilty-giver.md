# Profile C — The Guilty Giver
**Tagline:** Boundaries aren't selfish — they're sacred.
**Scripture:** Galatians 5:1

---

## Email 1 — Send Immediately After Assessment
**Subject:** Your Silence Profile is here, [First Name]
**Preview text:** Your heart to serve is beautiful. And it's become your prison.

---

[First Name],

You just completed The Quiet Authority assessment — and what it revealed about you is both tender and true.

You are **The Guilty Giver.**

Here's what that means:

Your heart for people is one of the most beautiful things about you. You show up. You serve. You give — even when you have nothing left. The people in your life are better because you exist.

And you are exhausted.

Not because you love too much. But because somewhere along the way, you confused love with self-abandonment. You started measuring your worth by how available you were to everyone else — and silence began to feel selfish. Like betrayal.

*"It is for freedom that Christ has set us free."*
— Galatians 5:1

He set you free, [First Name]. Not free to serve without limits until you disappear. Free. Fully. Completely free.

**Your first practice (start tonight):**
Before you respond to anyone today — one text, one request, one need — sit for 10 minutes first. Let God's voice be the first voice you hear. Not the need. Not the request. His voice.

Just that. Just tonight.

Boundaries aren't selfish. They're sacred. And you're about to discover why.

Walking with you,
**Grace**

## Email 2 — Send Day 3
**Subject:** The permission you've been waiting for someone to give you
**Preview text:** You are allowed to say no.

---

[First Name],

I'm going to give you something today that no one may have given you before:

**Permission.**

Permission to say no without a detailed explanation.
Permission to let someone be disappointed without fixing it.
Permission to protect your time, your energy, your silence — not someday, not when things slow down, but now.

You have been waiting for someone to tell you it's okay. So I'm telling you.

*"Am I now seeking the approval of man, or of God?"*
— Galatians 1:10

You cannot serve everyone and serve God. At some point, those two things are in direct conflict — and you have been choosing everyone else. Every time.

Here is what God is trying to tell you through the silence: **disappointing people is not the same as disappointing Him.**

Some of the people you've been trying not to disappoint? He never asked you to carry them. That was you — your guilt, your fear, your beautiful broken need to be needed.

**This week's practice:**
Identify one "yes" in your life that should be a "no." Don't cancel it yet. Just name it. Sit with it for 10 minutes in silence and ask: *Is this what God is asking of me, or what someone else is?*

The answer will come.

Still with you,
**Grace**

**Preview text:** This is what giving was always meant to feel like.

One week of practicing something radical: putting God's voice before everyone else's needs.

Here's what I want you to notice: **when you serve from fullness, it feels different.**

Not depleted. Not resentful. Not that low-grade guilt that never quite goes away. Full. Present. Actually giving — not just enduring.

*"Give, and it will be given to you. Good measure, pressed down, shaken together, running over."*

Running over. That's the version of you that God designed. Not the empty version scraping the bottom to give one more thing to one more person. The overflowing version — so full of what He's poured in that what you give doesn't cost you your soul.

You can only get there through the silence. Through the practice of filling up before you pour out.

You've started that practice, [First Name]. Don't stop.
