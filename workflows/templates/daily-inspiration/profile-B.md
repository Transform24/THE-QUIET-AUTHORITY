# Daily Inspiration — Profile B: The Depleted Survivor
*7-day rotation · Fill in full content before activating Agent 06*

---

## Day 1
**Scripture:** "He heals the brokenhearted and binds up their wounds." — Psalm 147:3 (NIV)

**Practice:** Place one hand over your heart and breathe slowly for two minutes, doing nothing else. You do not have to pray words — let the breath itself be the prayer, and let the hand on your heart be the tenderness you have not known how to give yourself.

**Inspiration:** You have survived things that were never supposed to happen to you, and surviving has taken everything. But there is a God who sees the full cost — not just what broke, but what it required of you to keep going — and He is not standing at a distance admiring your resilience. He is close, binding what is still tender. You are allowed to let Him.

---

## Day 2
**Scripture:** "The Lord is close to the brokenhearted and saves those who are crushed in spirit." — Psalm 34:18 (NIV)

**Practice:** Find a quiet corner and sit with the lights low, or with your eyes closed, for five minutes. You do not need to speak or produce anything. This is not meditation with an outcome — it is simply practicing the belief that God is near, even in the quiet, even in the dark.

**Inspiration:** When a woman has been through what you have been through, she sometimes learns to keep people at arm's length to stay safe — and that instinct protected you when you needed it. But God is not one more person who will leave when it costs them something. He presses in close to the crushed places. He chooses them. He chose you there, in the hardest season, and He has not moved.

---

## Day 3
**Scripture:** "He gives strength to the weary and increases the power of the weak." — Isaiah 40:29 (NIV)

**Practice:** Name — aloud or in writing — one thing that took strength from you this week, and one thing, however small, that reminded you of God's presence. Hold both without trying to resolve them. Some days faith is simply the willingness to hold the contradiction without walking away.

**Inspiration:** The strength God gives the weary does not look like the strength you had before. It is quieter, gentler, more like a deep root than a strong branch — it holds you from beneath rather than pushing you forward. That is the kind of strength He is building in you now, in this season you did not choose. It will not announce itself loudly. But it will not fail you.

---

## Day 4
**Scripture:** "Even to your old age and gray hairs I am he, I am he who will sustain you. I have made you and I will carry you; I will sustain you and I will rescue you." — Isaiah 46:4 (NIV)

**Practice:** Write down one burden you have been carrying alone — not to share it with anyone, just to name it on paper so it is outside of you for a moment. Then write next to it: "He will carry this." You do not have to feel that it is true for it to be true. Write it anyway.

**Inspiration:** Surviving has trained you to carry, to manage, to stay ahead of the next hard thing. But the God who made you did not design you to be your own sustainer — He claimed that role as His own. "I will carry you" is not a future promise you have to earn your way toward. It is a present declaration from a God who has already been carrying you through everything that should have broken you and didn't.

---

## Day 5
**Scripture:** "He restores my soul." — Psalm 23:3a (NIV)

**Practice:** Spend five minutes today with something that once brought you joy before the hard season — a song, a scent, a texture, a place — and let yourself simply be in it without analyzing whether you feel anything. Restoration begins in the body as much as the spirit. Show up for it.

**Inspiration:** Restoration is not the same as return — you cannot go back to who you were before, and He is not asking you to. He is doing something new, something that honors the wounds without being defined by them. Your soul is not beyond repair; it is already in His hands. The shepherd who left the ninety-nine to find the one lost sheep is the same God who will not stop the work of restoring what has been lost in you.

---

## Day 6
**Scripture:** "For I know the plans I have for you, declares the Lord, plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future." — Jeremiah 29:11 (NIV)

**Practice:** Read this verse as if it were spoken directly to you — because it was. Then write down one word that represents the future you are afraid to hope for. Hold that word before God today, not as a demand but as a quiet offering: this is what I barely dare to want. Show me if it is from You.

**Inspiration:** These words were spoken to people in exile — people who had lost everything and were wondering if God had forgotten them. He had not. He will not forget you. Whatever was taken from you, whatever season of exile you have endured, the God who knows the end from the beginning has not stopped planning good things over your life. Hope is not naive here. Hope is the prophetic act of a woman who knows whose hands she is in.

---

## Day 7
**Scripture:** "And the God of all grace, who called you to his eternal glory in Christ, after you have suffered a little while, will himself restore you and make you strong, firm and steadfast." — 1 Peter 5:10 (NIV)

**Practice:** Read this verse slowly and mark the word "himself" — not an angel, not a process, not time alone. Then spend three minutes in silent gratitude for what He has already restored, even if it is small. Gratitude is not denial of what was lost. It is the practice of noticing what has survived and what is slowly returning.

**Inspiration:** "After you have suffered a little while." God does not pretend the suffering was nothing — He names it, acknowledges its weight, and then speaks restoration over what comes after. The suffering was real. The restoration is also real, and it comes from His own hands. You are not too far gone, too long broken, or too deeply scarred for the God of all grace to reach. He called you. He is completing what He started.
